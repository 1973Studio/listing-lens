Scanned 22 Dec 2025 · Analysis based on 1 screenshot

Lens Score
94/100
Verdict

Proceed with caution — verify compliance items and confirm mechanical history before money changes hands.

Visual & Technical Forensics

  • Seller claims don’t fully match detected trim/spec — confirm exact variant.
  • Modifications appear present; verify parts, receipts, and install quality.

Regulatory & Legal (AU)

  • Any lift/tyres/exhaust changes may require certification — confirm engineering paperwork.
  • If it’s modified, confirm insurance will cover it. Don’t assume.

Mechanical Risk

  • Check service history continuity and receipts (not just a stamp).
  • Ask for recall status proof where applicable.

Ownership Reality

The “cool mods” usually come with noise, comfort tradeoffs, running costs, and insurance friction. Budget for that.
